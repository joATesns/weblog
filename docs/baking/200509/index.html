The recipe is settling

The recipe is settling

With the experiments of the lasts week(s), I’m tuning the recipe. Currently the one that I’m experimenting with is:

The preferment

  • 20 grams of rye flour
  • 100 grams of other flour
  • 120 grams of water
  • 24 grams of starter

The complete build

  • 116 grams of rye flour
  • 564 grams of other flour
  • 520 grams of water
  • 18 grams of salt

There are still plenty of parameters to toy around with, but at least I have some base to start from. I’ll do the math for you:

  • there is 800 grams of flour
  • hydration is 80%
  • 2% - 2.5% of salt

The other flour

I’m doing some new experiments with the other flour. This could be whole grain or white flour. Let’s try 3 different scenario’s: 100% whole grain, 75% whole grain, and fifty-fifty.

Conclusions

  1. I’m no photographer (sorry dad, who is a retired, professional photographer)
  2. Kids prefer the more white (taste is getting sweeter)
  3. The color of the outside crust doesn’t reflect the composition of the inside
  4. The larger the share of white flour, the better the rise

Maybe I should redo this experiment without the rye flour. It gives an offset to the ratio of white and whole grain.
